# Introduction to Aerospace Manufacturing
Study Guide for Intro to Aerospace Manufacturing

### What is this Study Guide?
The Washington Aerospace Training & Research Center (WATR) offers five specialty 12-week programs to launch a new career as an aircraft mechanic.
Top notch instructors provide hands on training in fully functional lab/shop sitting adjacent to Boeing's giant Everett manufacturing plant at Paine Field.
The "Aerospace Manufacturing Core" class kicks off the program.  It is a 9-credit, 4-week, online-class ending in a proctored, onsite exam.
This study guide complements that core class, a review of the basic fundamentals.

### Why was this Created?
**Levelset:** My day job involves data analysis, business intelligence (reporting), and programming in the aerospace manufacturing industry.
**The Problem:** Needed to start filling in fundamental gaps in basic industry knowledge.  Also want to share with my teammates the same knowledge.  Also want to share back with future students so the have better visuals.
**The Solution:** Spent $2,500 and 41 hours taking the classes, studying the material and passing the exam.  After that, spent 145 hours creating this slide deck study guide.  Did this on my own dime and time.
Heavily used Google AI for words, and ChatGPT for images (see notes behinds the slides to learn how).  Referenced every single image in the guide with either a government public domain source, or a CC0/3/4 creative commons license...goal being to make the info shareable so WATR folks can take and use it however they want, and I can use it for brown bag trainings at work to help others.

### How Do I Use It?
* Open the ".ppsx" files directly from here in a web browser, use mouse-pointer or arrow keys to move backward or forward in the slide show (requires PowerPoint)
* Drop down into the "PowerPoint Source Files" folder, and open each ".pptx" file with PowerPoint to edit the content yourself (same files both places, just different formats)
* If you do not have PowerPoint, PDF versions are saved in the "PDF Files" folder
* Note that there are (3) "100" series course guides that cover the high level "Intro to Aerospace Manufacturing" material
* Note that there are (6) "200" series course guides that cover in detail "Tools and Processes"
* Note that there are (5) "300" series course guides that cover in detail "Blueprints and Documentation" -- my personal favorite that I want to share with teammates during brown bag lunch trainings
